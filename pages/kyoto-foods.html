The 7 Must-Try Foods in Kyoto

By Sebastian Santillan | Published on June 15, 2024

Kyoto, celebrated as Japan's cultural and historical heart, also boasts a culinary tradition that mirrors its rich heritage. From elegant kaiseki meals to humble street snacks, Kyoto offers a spectrum of flavors that captivate both locals and visitors alike. In this guide, we embark on a gastronomic journey through Kyoto's must-try foods, inviting you to savor the essence of this ancient capital through its delectable dishes.

1. Kaiseki:

  • Description: Kaiseki is the pinnacle of Kyoto's culinary artistry, a multi-course dining experience that elevates seasonal ingredients to a form of culinary poetry.
  • Where to Try: Indulge in the refined ambiance of Michelin-starred restaurants like Kikunoi and Hyotei, where each dish is crafted to evoke the essence of the season.
  • Experience: From delicate sashimi to exquisitely prepared tempura and meticulously arranged desserts, kaiseki is a feast for the senses that embodies Kyoto's dedication to culinary excellence and aesthetics.

2. Yudofu (Tofu Hot Pot):

  • Description: Yudofu epitomizes Kyoto's commitment to simplicity and purity, featuring tofu simmered in a delicate kombu broth and served with dipping sauces and seasonal vegetables.
  • Where to Try: Experience authentic yudofu at tranquil settings like Nanzenji Junsei, where the serene surroundings complement the dish's subtle flavors.
  • Experience: Enjoy the velvety texture of tofu paired with the umami-rich broth, a dish that reflects Kyoto's Zen Buddhist heritage and reverence for natural ingredients.

3. Kyoto Sweets (Wagashi):

  • Description: Wagashi are traditional Japanese sweets that harmonize sweetness with artistic craftsmanship, often enjoyed alongside tea ceremonies.
  • Where to Try: Visit revered confectioneries such as Tsuruya Yoshinobu and Nakamura Tokichi, where master artisans create seasonal wagashi that celebrate Kyoto's cultural rituals.
  • Experience: Enjoy the velvety texture of tofu paired with the umami-rich broth, a dish that reflects Kyoto's Zen Buddhist heritage and reverence for natural ingredients.

4. Kyoto Sweets (Wagashi):

  • Description: Wagashi are traditional Japanese sweets that harmonize sweetness with artistic craftsmanship, often enjoyed alongside tea ceremonies.
  • Where to Try: Visit revered confectioneries such as Tsuruya Yoshinobu and Nakamura Tokichi, where master artisans create seasonal wagashi that celebrate Kyoto's cultural rituals.
  • Experience: Delight in the intricate designs and subtle flavors of wagashi, from soft mochi to smooth yokan, each bite a testament to Kyoto's tea culture and refined taste.

5. Obanzai:

  • Description: Obanzai embodies Kyoto's home-style cooking, featuring a variety of small dishes that highlight locally sourced ingredients and emphasize seasonal flavors.
  • Where to Try: Dive into the flavors of Kyoto at establishments like Kamo Deli Obanzai Café and Obanzai & Sake Yuyu, where obanzai meals offer a taste of everyday Kyoto life.
  • Experience: Sample an array of pickled vegetables, simmered fish, and tofu preparations that showcase Kyoto's culinary philosophy of balance, health, and respect for nature.

6. Matcha (Green Tea):

  • Description: Kyoto is renowned for its premium matcha, a finely ground green tea powder celebrated for its health benefits and versatile culinary uses.
  • Where to Try: Immerse yourself in the world of matcha at iconic tea houses like Ippodo Tea and explore Uji, Kyoto's historic tea-growing region.
  • Experience: From traditional tea ceremonies to matcha-flavored sweets and beverages, savor the distinctive umami flavor and vibrant green hue that define Kyoto's matcha culture.

7. Yuba (Tofu Skin):

  • Description: Yuba, the delicate skin that forms on the surface of heated soy milk, showcases Kyoto's innovation in tofu-based dishes.
  • Where to Try: Discover the versatility of yuba at Seike Yuba, offering everything from fresh yuba sashimi to hearty yuba hot pots.
  • Experience: Appreciate the delicate texture and subtle sweetness of yuba, often served with soy sauce or incorporated into Kyoto's traditional cuisine, highlighting the city's dedication to culinary craftsmanship.

8. Kyo-ryori (Kyoto Cuisine):

  • Description: Kyo-ryori is Kyoto's refined cuisine, characterized by its emphasis on fresh, seasonal ingredients and meticulous preparation techniques.
  • Where to Try: Indulge in the elegance of Kyo-ryori at esteemed restaurants such as Gion Kappa and Manzara Honten, where every dish is a tribute to Kyoto's culinary heritage.
  • Experience: From delicate shojin ryori to perfectly grilled fish and exquisite vegetable tempura, Kyo-ryori invites you to savor Kyoto's flavors with every bite, embodying centuries of tradition and culinary excellence.

Conclusion

Kyoto's culinary landscape is a tapestry woven with tradition, innovation, and a deep appreciation for nature's bounty. Whether you're indulging in the artistic precision of kaiseki or savoring the comforting simplicity of yudofu, each dish offers a glimpse into Kyoto's cultural soul. Embark on this culinary adventure to discover why Kyoto's gastronomy is as revered as its temples and gardens, and let these must-try foods create unforgettable memories of your journey through this ancient city.
